Resolving "NIK needs verification" during BPJS sync

AW
Andi Wijaya
Updated 19 May 2026 · 4 min read · 1,847 views

BPJS Ketenagakerjaan sync may fail for one or more employees with the error NIK needs verification. This happens when Dukcapil (Indonesia's national civil registry) flags a NIK for re-verification — most commonly because of an address change, name update, or stale KTP photo.

Why it happens

The BPJS API performs a real-time lookup against Dukcapil on every employee enrollment. If Dukcapil returns requires_verification, the BPJS API rejects the sync until the citizen completes verification in person at their local Disdukcapil office.

Note: This is a Dukcapil-side check — KantorCore cannot bypass it. The good news: re-verification is usually completed within 1–3 business days.

Fix it in 4 steps

  1. Open the affected employee record in HR → Employees.
  2. Check the BPJS Sync tab — you'll see the exact rejection reason.
  3. Ask the employee to visit their local Disdukcapil office. They need to bring a current KTP and complete a fingerprint scan.
  4. Once Disdukcapil updates the record (you can verify at dukcapil.kemendagri.go.id), come back to KantorCore and click Retry sync on the employee record.

Bulk handling

If you have multiple employees with the same issue, you can bulk-export the list and share it with HR. From the BPJS sync log, click Export failed → CSV. You can then send the list as a single email to your HR team.
